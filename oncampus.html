Block M on the Diag

Located on the University's diag near hatcher graduate library and other university building, the Block M serves as the university's core. Ther is a legend that if a student steps on the Block M, they will fail their exams and will have bad luck. Make sure you watch your step when you visit the Block M.







The Cube

"The Cube" is located directly outside of the north side of the union and on the east side of the LSA building on central campus. It is said that the cube gets spun by the university's president in order for the day to go smoothly here at the University of Michigan. There is a myth that if the Cube is spun in a particular direction, then a student who does so will have good luck. If you are wondering, yes it is heavy.







Burton Memorial Tower

One of the best sites to see on the University of Michigan's central campus. The clocktower is located in Ingalls Mall which is surrounded by the MLB, the league, and the hill auditorium. You can't miss it! Anyone is able to tour the clocktower on weekdays after noon and go to the top. Feel free to stop by and check it out! Fun Fact: Believe it or not some classes are actually held in the clocktower.











The Big House

The one and only Big House in the Nation. It is the largest stadium in the nation and tends to have 100,000+ fans on average for football game days. Tours are offered here and there and its an experience you will never forget. The big house is furthur south of central campus. Neighboring the Big House is the Crisler Center which is where Michigan Basketball and Volleyball games are held. Theres always great energy, thrill, and fun to be had at the Big House!











Law Quad

Possibly one of the most beautiful things one could ever see on campus. Law quad is located right off South University Street near State Street. It is glorious and a fantastic place to take photos and make memories. When it is warmer outside, many student tend to chill outside on the law quad to either do work or just relax from classes. Law Quad is at it's best when it rains a bit and the lighting is just right. Feel free to check it out, but try to make as little noise as possible for the law students studying hard!











University of Michigan Museum of Art

This is one of the Museums that are on the University of Michigan's central campus. The University of Michigan Museum of Art (UMMA) is located near the corner of South University and State street. It is also directly across the street from the Michigan Union. UMMA has a lot to offer such as, collections in African Arts, sculptures, Asian Artifacts, and lots more. Best of all, the museum is completely free to enter and explore!














University of Michigan Museum of Natural History

This is another one of the University of Michigan's Museums that are on campus. The Natural history Museum is located near the hill right off of washtenaw avenue and can be accessed from the Central Campus Transit Center (CCTC) side. The museum itself feature lots of fossils and consists of lots of natural sciences.












Wave Field

Wave field is located on the University's North campus right inbetween the FXB and Gorguze Family Lab (GFL). It may be a bit far but you cannot miss it! There are many large slopes of grass in this area that resembles waves.



Giant Rubik's Cube

This giant Rubik's cube is also located on noth campus in GGBL. GGBL is a bit large and confusing but seeing this cube will make your day! The cube has been aaround since 2017 and it can be solved!


Lurie Bell Tower

The Robert H. Lurie Tower is named in honor of a University of Michigan alumnus that was diagnosed with cancer but pathed the way towards improvements in cancer research. The tower itseld is on the North Campus Grove and ocassionally will play a bell jingle. You cannot miss this!
